Mission Statement

Our mission is to restore degraded ecosystems through the wholistic use of natural processes

This non-profit looks to employ local initiatives as well as represent geographical data online that will affect the Tampa Bay area in a positive way.

The red tide has caused devastating effects to our coasts; however, this project seeks to control eutrophication and improve the quality of the Bay’s water through bivalve aquaculture.

Terra Corp is a 501(3)(c) non-profit whose goal is to agriculturally produce indigenous oysters while also providing the local food pyramid with much needed primary consumers.

Terra Corp would employ students as a source for semi-educated labor while providing practical education. On a larger scale the ambition would be to work in conjunction with other local non-profits (UACDC) in job placement to help employ lower income individuals with consistent work. This business model has already been proven successful as only one family owned for profit company operates in the Tampa Bay area.

Nutrient Dynamics in the Tampa Bay Watershed

Tampa bay is a diverse area ranging from a dense urban downtown to suburban and resort locations. This development diversity requires Tampa to have equally intricate legislation to maintain the quality of the watershed it resides in. Quite often the bay and beaches are subject to massive algal blooms, some more toxic than others.
